The Research

Grounded in national data

Nudge is informed by peer-reviewed research using the 2019 All India Debt and Investment Survey (AIDIS) — one of the most comprehensive datasets on household indebtedness in India, conducted by India's National Sample Survey Office.

The study analysed 480,687 loans across rural and urban households, applying OLS and Logit regression with state and district-level fixed effects to identify the household characteristics most strongly associated with formal credit access.

The findings reveal that inequalities in India's credit market closely mirror broader social and structural inequalities — and that broader financial development alone will not automatically translate into equal access.

How It Works

Three steps to a better loan

1
Share your situation
Tell Nudge about the loan you're considering — the amount, tenure, lender type, and interest rate if known. Optionally share household details for a personalised credit access profile based on AIDIS research.
2
We analyse your options
Nudge cross-references regulated MFI rates in your district and uses AIDIS research to contextualise your credit access. You get a clear cost comparison — interest, total repayment, and monthly payment — in rupees.
3
Make an informed choice
Nudge surfaces verified contact details for regulated alternatives. You decide — Nudge just makes sure you have the information to compare before you commit.
