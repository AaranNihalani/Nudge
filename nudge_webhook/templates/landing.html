Key Findings

Who gets excluded from formal credit

Average Marginal Effects from Logit regression with district fixed effects (Table 5, Model 5). Each figure is independent — not additive. pp = percentage points change in probability of accessing formal credit.

AME Group Finding
−10.7 pp Muslim vs Hindu Largest religion-linked gap in the study. Persists after income and location controls.
−6.2 pp SC vs OBC Scheduled Caste households, controlling for income and geography.
+6.2 pp "Other" caste vs OBC Upper-caste households. Caste predicts access independently of income.
−3.7 pp ST vs OBC Scheduled Tribe households. Geographic remoteness compounds the gap.
−3.2 pp Christian vs Hindu Statistically significant. Likely reflects regional concentration effects.
+2.5 pp Per acre of land Land acts as implicit collateral, better recognised by regulated lenders.
+2.3 pp Urban vs rural Proximity to banking infrastructure. Narrows with district FE.
+1.1 pp Per ₹1,000 MPCE Monthly per-capita expenditure. Strongest individual-level predictor.
−0.6 pp Per household member Larger households marginally less likely to access formal credit.

Grounded in national data

How it works

Three steps

01
Set your district
Tell Nudge your district and describe the loan — amount, tenure, rate if known. Answer any household questions (caste, income, land) for a personalised profile, or skip.
02
See the real cost
Nudge cross-references regulated MFI rates in your district and computes total interest, total repayment, and monthly payment in rupees — ranked by APR.
03
Compare before you commit
Nudge surfaces contact details for regulated alternatives. Your profile is saved — when you return, you pick up where you left off. You decide.
