The Research

Grounded in national data

Nudge is informed by peer-reviewed research using the 2019 All India Debt and Investment Survey (AIDIS) — one of the most comprehensive datasets on household indebtedness in India, conducted by India's National Sample Survey Office across 5,940 villages and 3,995 urban blocks.

The study analysed 480,687 loan records across rural and urban households, applying OLS and Logit regression with state and district-level fixed effects to identify the household characteristics most strongly associated with formal credit access.

Crucially, the analysis controls for income, location, and loan purpose — isolating the independent effects of caste, religion, household size, and landholding on the probability of accessing regulated credit. The findings reveal that inequalities in India's credit market closely mirror its broader social inequalities.

Nudge converts these Average Marginal Effects into actionable, personalised guidance: if your household characteristics are associated with lower formal credit access, you get the alternatives most likely to work for you first.

Who Nudge Helps

Built for real situations

Nudge is designed for households who are already being approached by informal lenders — or who have already borrowed — and need a fast, low-friction way to understand their alternatives before they commit.

🏥
Medical emergency
39.8% of informal loans are for medical expenses. Nudge surfaces district-specific MFI alternatives that can disburse quickly — often the same day.
🌾
Agricultural input
Rural households with seasonal income often borrow at high rates for seeds, fertiliser, or equipment. Nudge identifies regulated crop-loan alternatives.
🏠
Home repair or construction
Small home improvement loans are disproportionately informal. Nudge compares structured MFI housing loans by APR and repayment term.
📊
Checking an existing loan
Already borrowed at a high rate? Send the terms to Nudge and get a clear comparison of what you're paying vs. what regulated lenders charge in your district.
How It Works

Three steps to a better loan

1
Set your district, share your situation
Tell Nudge your district and the loan you're considering — the amount, tenure, lender type, and interest rate if known. Optionally share household details (caste, income, land) for a personalised credit access profile based on AIDIS research. You can answer "don't know" to any question.
2
Get a clear cost breakdown
Nudge cross-references regulated MFI rates in your district and computes the actual cost in rupees — total interest, total repayment, and monthly payment. You see a ranked list of alternatives sorted by APR so you can compare before committing.
3
Make an informed choice
Nudge surfaces verified contact details for regulated alternatives in your area. Your profile is saved — when you return, you pick up where you left off. You decide: Nudge just ensures you have the information to compare.
