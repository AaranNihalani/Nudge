Key Findings

Who gets left out of formal credit

Each figure is an average marginal effect from logit regression with district fixed effects: the change in probability of accessing formal credit for that group, holding all else equal. Estimates are independent and should not be summed.

Effect Group Notes
-10.7 pp Muslim vs Hindu The largest religion-linked gap in the data. Holds after controlling for income, location, and household size.
-6.2 pp SC vs OBC Scheduled Caste households, after controlling for income and geography.
+6.2 pp Upper caste vs OBC The gap runs in both directions; caste predicts credit access independently of income.
-3.7 pp ST vs OBC Scheduled Tribe households. Geographic remoteness and social exclusion appear to compound.
-3.2 pp Christian vs Hindu Smaller but statistically significant. Likely reflects regional concentration patterns.
+2.5 pp Per acre of land owned Land is recognised as implicit collateral more readily by regulated lenders than informal ones.
+2.3 pp Urban vs rural Proximity to bank branches and MFI offices remains significant after district-level controls.
+1.1 pp Per ₹1,000 monthly income The strongest individual-level predictor. Each additional ₹1,000 per person per month is associated with higher formal credit likelihood.
-0.6 pp Per additional household member Larger households are marginally less likely to access formal credit, possibly owing to lower per-capita collateral or income variability.
The Research

Built on peer-reviewed findings

Nudge is the applied extension of published research on credit market inequality in India. Household profile data is used to retrieve the relevant estimates from the paper and present personalised, evidence-based comparisons.

Publication details
Journal International Journal of High School Research, June 2026
Preprint SSRN Working Paper 6006354
Data AIDIS 2019, 480,687 loans, all-India
Coverage 5,940 villages, 3,995 urban blocks
Methods OLS and logit regression, average marginal effects, district fixed effects

How it works

Three steps

01
Enter your loan details
Set your district, then describe the loan: amount, tenure, and lender type. Nudge asks six short questions about your household to personalise the output. Any question can be skipped.
02
Get a cost breakdown
Nudge calculates total interest, total repayment, and monthly payment for your loan, then compares these against regulated MFI rates in your district, ordered by APR.
03
Compare and choose
Regulated alternatives are listed with contact details. Your profile is saved, so returning users pick up where they left off. No account or sign-up is required.
