Key Findings

What the research says about formal credit access in Thailand

Rows marked Own analysis come from regression on the Townsend Thai Project Annual Resurvey 2017 (N=1,200 rural households, 4 provinces; Harvard Dataverse doi:10.7910/DVN/UW4VKE). Rows marked Published are directional findings from the cited peer-reviewed papers — not original analysis by this project.
Effect Group Notes
+0.26 pp Per additional household memberOwn analysis Each extra household member is associated with +0.26 pp higher probability of holding a formal loan (p=0.043, OLS-LPM, HC3 SEs). Larger households face greater capital needs and may have stronger social networks. Townsend Thai 2017 Annual Resurvey, rural panel.
+0.25 pp Per additional household member (informal loans)Own analysis Household size predicts informal loan holding too (+0.25 pp, p=0.027) — not a substitution effect. Larger families borrow more across both channels. Townsend Thai 2017 Annual Resurvey.
−0.51 pp Northeast vs Central region (informal loans)Own analysis Northeast households (Sisaket, Buriram) are 0.51 pp less likely to hold informal loans than Central households (p=0.007). Informal credit markets appear thinner in the Northeast — fewer lender networks, lower income density. Townsend Thai 2017 Annual Resurvey.
Lower Unemployed / freelance / retirees vs salariedPublished Without verifiable income documentation, informal workers cannot meet formal lender requirements. This group disproportionately relies on loan sharks. Srisawad et al. (2025) JRFM 18(11):632, N=6,949, 77 provinces.
Higher Farming households vs non-farmingPublished Farming households have better formal credit access. BAAC agricultural loan programmes and land as collateral are the primary channels. Srisawad et al. (2025) JRFM 18(11):632.
Higher North / Northeast vs Bangkok (formal, national survey)Published In the national 77-province survey, North and Northeast households are more likely to access formal finance than Bangkok residents. Bangkok's deep informal market reduces the formal-channel advantage for lower-income urban workers. Srisawad et al. (2025) JRFM 18(11):632.
Higher rate Stronger lender social influence over borrower (Bangkok metro)Published In the Bangkok metropolitan area, greater lender influence over the borrower is independently associated with higher informal interest rates — regardless of loan size or income. Tanomchat & Sampattavanija (2018) Int. Adv. Econ. Res. 24(1):47–63, N=694.
